Project Intention

‘Emblem Status’ is an artistic intervention that focuses on the status of emblems and sculptures on house facades. Wealthy institutions have the possibility to establish themselves in the historic houses of the city. While the beautiful emblems are therefore used as decoration, they also represent the monetary value and status of the institution inside of the walls. We wanted to open up this exclusivity and bring it into real public space. By taking physical impressions of the emblems with clay, we extracted the forms and textures as they are at Paradeplatz. This act represents our need to simply display the situation of reality. The clay serves as a mould for the plaster mass, which results in the copied emblem of the house facade in plaster. The plaster emblems are distributed to people on the streets for free. This performance symbolizes the distribution of status and wealth of thes institutions.

About the Local Material: Würenloser Muschelkalk,

The emblems of the facade, from which we took the impressions, are originally made of ‘Würenloser Muschelkalk’. The quarries near Würenlos supplied the Limmattal with building materials throughout the Middle Ages and in the centuries that followed. The project was preceded by initial tests in which we extracted clay-likely material from the local "Limmat" river, which closes the symbolic connection of the actual Material of the sculptures and its taken mould of clay.

The Other Nefertiti – Artists release the 3D data of Nefertitis Head.

Nefertiti is returning to the place where it was found. For the first time since the sculpture was excavated and stolen over 100 years ago, the Other Nefertiti visits Egypt for the first time.

"The Other Nefertiti" is an artistic intervention by the two German artists Nora Al-Badri and Jan Nikolai Nelles.

Al-Badri and Nelles scanned the head of Nefertiti clandestinely in the Neues Museum Berlin without permission of the Museum and they hereby announce the release of the 3D data of Nefertitis head under a Creative Commons Licence.
